Kashi

History of Kashi

Kashi is one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. It is considered one of the holiest cities in Hinduism and is a major pilgrimage site.

Kashi, also known as Varanasi or Benaras, is a city on the Ganges river in northern India. It holds a central place in the traditions of pilgrimage, death, and mourning in the Hindu world.

Here are some key details about Kashi:

Historical Significance: Kashi is one of the oldest living cities in the world. It is considered to be the holiest city in Hinduism and is a major pilgrimage site for Hindus from all over the world.

Religious Importance: Kashi is believed to be the eternal abode of Lord Shiva. The Kashi Vishwanath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples, is dedicated to him.

Cultural Significance: Varanasi has been referred to as Kashi in Rig Veda, one of the most ancient Hindu sacred texts. The term Kashi is derived from the words Kaś that means ‘to shine’. Hence, Kashi is also known as the ‘city of light’.

Geographical Location: Varanasi is located at coordinates 25°19′08″N83°00′46″E. It is situated in the state of Uttar Pradesh, India.
